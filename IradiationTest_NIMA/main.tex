\begin{abstract}
\begin{linenumbers}
LHC-ATLAS is the energy frontier experiment for the purpose of studying known mechanisms in greater detail and searching for new physics. This experiment has recorded the data corresponding to the total integrated luminosity of 196  $\rm{fb}^{-1}$ from 2010. For advancing the search in the field of energy frontier, High luminosity LHC (HL-LHC), the luminosity will be increased, will start in 2029. HL-LHC will record the data corresponding to the total integrated luminosity 3000  $\rm{fb}^{-1}$ for 10 years. Due to increasing the luminosity, the electronics installed close to the detector are required to have high radiation hardness. The frontend electronics of the Thin Gap Chamber (TGC), which is the endcap muon detector of the ATLAS experiments, are located about 10 m from the collision point. The radiation level of the frontend electronics are estimated 4.1 - 4.3  Gy for Total Ionizing Dose (TID), 1.1 - 2.2 $\times 10^{11}$ 1-MeV neutron equivalent flux for Non-Ionizing Energy Loss (NIEL). Frontend electronics have a lot of commercial electronic components. So the test of radiation hardness of these components, in which the radiation hardness is not obvious, is the key point for HL-LHC. This paper shows the results of TID and NIEL tests for Voltage references, SD cards, Operational Amplifiers, Clock jitter cleaners, QSPI flash memories, SFP+ transceivers, Optical fiber, and LDOs. For TID tests, we irradiated gamma rays up to O(1000)  Gy at the Cobalt-60 facility of Nagoya University. For NIEL tests, we irradiated neutrons up to O($10^{12}$)  $\rm{cm}^{-2}$ using Tandem Accelerator at Kobe University. As the results of these tests, we verified all irradiated commercial electronic components have enough radiation hardness in terms of use for TGC frontend electronics at HL-LHC.  
\end{linenumbers}
\end{abstract}
